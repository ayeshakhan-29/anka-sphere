# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The internal team of a digital agency — ten roles across three departments (Product Modelling, Development, Growth): managers, content writers, designers, developers, social/paid/SEO specialists. They live in this tool daily at their desks, moving client projects through a five-stage delivery pipeline. Context: focused work sessions, frequent context-switching between projects, quick status checks between meetings.

## Product Purpose

Anka Sphere replaces scattered coordination (chat threads, duplicate uploads, chasing briefs) with one operations platform. Client projects flow through five gated stages — Profiling → Written Content → Design → Development → Marketing — and every department sees where everything stands. Success: a team member can answer "what's the state of project X and what do I owe it?" in under ten seconds.

## Brand Personality

Calm, professional, precise. A dependable instrument, not a showpiece: quiet surfaces that let project data lead, with sharp, data-forward density where the work happens (kanbans, trackers, queues). Confidence through clarity, never through decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Generic admin templates** (AdminLTE / Bootstrap dashboards): endless identical stat cards, icon-heading-text grids, decorative charts.
- **Playful consumer apps**: rounded-everything, emoji-driven, gamified cuteness. This is a professional's daily tool.

## Design Principles

1. **The pipeline is the plot.** Every project view answers "which stage, what's blocking, who's next" before anything else.
2. **Departments have hue, the chrome stays quiet.** Department accent colors (design violet, social pink, paid red, SEO green…) are wayfinding on an otherwise neutral shell — never decoration for its own sake.
3. **Dense, never cramped.** Daily-use screens earn information density, but rhythm and whitespace keep scanning effortless.
4. **One system everywhere.** Sixteen pages, one token vocabulary. A button, badge, or table looks the same in every department.
5. **State is honest.** Loading, empty, and error states are designed deliberately — an empty queue tells you what to do next, not just that nothing is there.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum and AXE-clean (mandated in CLAUDE.md). Visible keyboard focus everywhere, ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast, ARIA-labelled interactive elements, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for all animation. Dark mode is a first-class theme, not an afterthought.
